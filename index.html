Key takeaways

Biggest strength

Peer connection was the runaway theme — GMs valued seeing colleagues as people, not just rankings. Random breakout groupings were specifically praised for sparking fresh perspectives.

Top opportunity

Venue (4.15) and food (3.93) remain the two weakest scores. Multiple respondents suggested a location change and better catering. A 2-day format was the most-requested structural change.

Content demand

P&L depth is the #1 topic request by a wide margin — respondents want more time, more detail, and a true class format. Power BI, Win Score, and labor/scheduling also surfaced repeatedly.

Discussion management

Multiple respondents flagged that some participants dominated discussions. Structured facilitation or timed contributions would help keep sessions equitable and on track.

Recognition & awards

The awards ceremony was a top-cited favorite. Consider expanding categories (most improvement, best yearly avg) to recognize more attendees in a meaningful way.

Plus-one & afterparty

23 of 41 said bringing a plus-one enhanced their experience. Of afterparty attendees, 33 of 34 reported connecting with peers from other stores.

Overall assessment

A−

The 2026 RGM Conference was a strong success.

4.61 / 5 overall  ·  68% gave 5 stars  ·  41 respondents

With 68% of attendees awarding a perfect score and an overall average of 4.61 out of 5, the 2026 conference landed well above the threshold of a successful event. The data tells a clear story: RGMs came to learn, to be recognized, and to connect with peers they rarely see outside of performance rankings — and the conference delivered on all three. The breakout sessions were rated nearly identically across all three tracks (4.59–4.68), which is a strong signal that the content and facilitation were consistently valuable regardless of which session attendees prioritized. The awards dinner and karaoke afterparty both cleared 4.5, reinforcing that the social and recognition elements are just as important to attendees as the instructional content.

The two areas pulling the score down are consistent and actionable: food & beverage (3.93) and venue (4.15) are the only categories below 4.5, and both were volunteered unprompted in open-ended responses. These are logistical decisions, not programmatic ones — meaning they can be corrected for 2027 without changing the core format that attendees clearly value. The most meaningful improvement opportunity, however, is content depth. Across every open-ended question category, the #1 theme was a desire for more time and more rigor in the financial sessions. Attendees aren't asking for something different — they're asking for more of what they got, taught more thoroughly.

Recommended priorities for 2027

1

Upgrade venue and catering

Food & beverage is the lowest-rated category and the most-cited improvement in open-ended responses. A venue change — multiple respondents suggested Texas — paired with better catering would immediately lift the lowest scores.

2

Extend to a 2-day format with deeper financial content

The single most requested structural change. Longer sessions dedicated to P&L mechanics, Win Score, invoicing, and scorecards — taught as real classes, not semester reviews — would address the #1 content gap directly.

3

Introduce structured facilitation in breakouts

Several respondents noted that vocal participants dominated discussions, limiting learning for others. Timed contributions or a designated facilitator per group would ensure equitable airtime and protect the session value for quieter attendees.

4

Add a formal peer introduction segment

One of the most specific and actionable suggestions: a structured breakout where RGMs meet GMs from other areas, and ADs introduce themselves and their teams. "Create connection, not just competition" was the direct ask.

5

Expand award categories

The awards ceremony was a top favorite, and attendees want more people recognized. Adding categories like most improved, best yearly average, or best CP would broaden participation and deepen the recognition experience.
